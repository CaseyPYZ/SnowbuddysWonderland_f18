a brief intro

For this project, my plan is to develop a "cube based" AR game/playset.

This playset is a system created by a "hardware" part and "software" part. The hardware portion will be nine 3 * 3 * 3 inch cubes (number and size might change), one side of every one of them will have a fiducial marker. When recognized by p5, on screen each cube will be given a unique little "inner world" of its size. This innerworld would be some type of environment, either natrual or scientifically artificial, 9 different types in total. When placed next to each other (or when their distance in between is within a certain range), the cubes will effect each other in a certain way. If it's not too ambitious, I'd like to create a pre-modeled 3D character to "live" in this cube-based world. Its movements would be controled by a input source (keyboard / joystick / ...), or it could be put on "play on itsown" mode and would then wander around in its world built by the player.

If what's written above could all be accomplished, then I would move on and try to see if it's possible to let users created their own customized character.

This is still a rather raw idea at its early stage. I'll research more to hammer out more detailed goals and progress. Visuals helping to explain the idea will be posted in a short while.
